OFDM

Overview of OFDM technology

i) OFDM is a multiplexing technology which splits data stream into multiple narrow band streams and each of the smaller streams modulates a carrier.

ii) The receiver receives all the multiplexed streams of data and demultiplexes them to form the final signal.

iii) sidebands from each carriers overlap but they can be received without interference as they are orthogonal to each other.

iv) Although it has the advantage of high spectral efficiency, it is complex, expensive and sensitive to carrier frequency variations.
